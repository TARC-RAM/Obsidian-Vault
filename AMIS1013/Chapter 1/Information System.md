## What is an Information System.
Information System is an integrated combination of
- [[Hardware]]
- [[Software]]
- [[Data]]
- [[People]]
  [[Processes]]
that work together to capture, store, input, process data and to produce information to help operate, support and manage organization
## Functions of Information System
- Input - Accepts raw data from outside the system
- Process - Processes the raw data into information
- Output - Produces the results of the process (information)
- Storage - Stores data, information, programs
## Example 1 - Inventory Control System (Receiving Goods Module)
- Input function - Order and delivery (Supplier) details (eg. PO No, Item code, Quantities)
- Process function - Process the input data (ie. goods received data) and update the quantities in the inventory data store etc.
- Output function - The user (eg. store manager) retrieve the information about the inventory details.
- Storage function - Data (eg. goods received data) and information (updated inventory balance) stored for future use.
## Types of Organisations (Product)
- Supplier - Supply raw materials that flow through an organisation and finally to consumer.
- Manufacturer - Raw materials are manufactured into products.
- Wholesaler - These products are sold to other businesses at wholesale - that is, in large quantities.
- Retailer - The products then are resold at retail - that is, in smaller quantities to the consumer

## Business Functions - Examples
- Marketing Functions
	- Determine prices of products and services
	- Advertising and promotions of products and services
	- Carry out promotions
	- Carry out market surveys of customers needs or change in tastes and trends
	- Carry out market intelligence of competitors
- Sales Functions
	- Answer inquiries from customers
	- Create quotations for customers
	- Accept orders from customers eg. create stock and customer credit status
	- Collaborate with other departments for delivery of orders, billing and collecting payments.
- Production Functions
	- Production planning - Deciding what (Products) to produce, how much to produce and when to produce
	- Capacity planning - Determining capacity requirements (machines and human resources)
	- Determine materials requirements
	- Monitoring and control production progress
- Human Resource Functions
	- Assessment of staff requirement
	- Recruitment of staff 
	- Training and development
	- Payroll
	- Staff performance evaluation
- Accounting Functions
	- Record business transaction entries 
	- Maintain assets records
	- Maintain liabilities records
	- Reporting - Financial profitability (eg P&L) and financial state of business (eg. Balance sheets)
- Finance Functions
	- Carry out budgeting
	- Obtaining money needed by the business
		- Sale of goods and services
		- Investments made by the business
		- Banks and other institutions
	- Plan and control the use of money 
- Information Technology Functions
	- Analysis and Design of users needs
	- Develop software
	- Buy packages
	- Implementing eg. training, data migration
	- Operation - Assisting other functional areas (depts) on using IT
	- Maintenance
## Enterprise Systems (Characteristics)
- Integrated System - Single large system consisting of many modules eg. ERP, CRM, SCM systems.
- Process Integration - ES integrates business processes, databases and functional areas (internally and externally). Allows data flows (vertically and horizontally)
- Centralized - ES provides a centralized database storage for all users (intra or inter-companies)
- Data Sharing - ES allows users to share information seamlessly no matter where the data is located and used for decision making
- Purpose - ES automates business processes of the entire enterprise
